# QMS-001 - Quality Management System

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the quality management system approach for the T.N. Memorial School Digital Platform. It establishes the principles, governance, and control mechanisms used to maintain quality across development, testing, deployment, and operations.

---

# 2. Scope

This document applies to quality planning, review, validation, defect management, process oversight, release readiness, and continuous improvement activities for the repository. It covers frontend, backend, API, database, security, testing, and deployment workstreams.

The scope includes quality controls that support reliable delivery and maintainability throughout the software lifecycle.

---

# 3. Overview

A quality management system is required to ensure that the platform is developed and maintained in a controlled, repeatable, and auditable manner. Quality expectations should be defined early and applied throughout implementation, testing, and release processes.

The approach should remain aligned with the repository’s architecture, testing strategy, security expectations, and deployment procedures.

---

# 4. Detailed Design

## Quality Objectives

The quality management system should support:

- Consistent development and documentation practices.
- Controlled testing and validation activities.
- Secure and reliable release management.
- Defect identification, tracking, and resolution.
- Continuous improvement through review and lessons learned.

## Quality Governance

Quality governance should include review of requirements, design artifacts, implementation changes, test evidence, deployment readiness, and operational outcomes. Quality activities should be visible to the responsible technical and business stakeholders.

## Quality Metrics

The project should track quality measures such as defect trends, test coverage where practical, review completion, release issues, and post-deployment incidents. These indicators should be used to inform planning and process improvements.

## Improvement Cycle

Findings from reviews, testing, and operations should be used to improve standards, documentation, and delivery practices. The quality system should support continuous refinement rather than isolated corrective action.

---

# 5. Best Practices

- Define quality expectations alongside requirements and delivery milestones.
- Review quality evidence for every major release or change set.
- Keep process documentation current and usable.
- Prioritize root-cause analysis for recurring quality issues.
- Use testing, deployment, and monitoring results to strengthen control mechanisms.

---

# 6. Security Considerations

Quality management must preserve the integrity of sensitive information and operational controls. Quality processes should not introduce unsafe shortcuts, bypass review, or weaken validation requirements.

Additional security considerations include:

- Protecting quality records and evidence from unauthorized access.
- Review of security-related defects and release risks.
- Ensuring that improvements do not reduce the platform’s security posture.
